# Major Project

## International Student Application Analysis

### Project Overview

This project analyzed the **DePaul University International Student Application Dataset** to understand applicant trends, outreach effectiveness, and program demand.

The goal was to identify patterns within the application data and provide strategic recommendations to improve recruitment outreach and admissions outcomes.


## Tools Used

* Excel
* Python
* Power BI


# Key Insights and Findings

## Insight 1: Majority of Applicants Come from Non-Study Group Channels

**Observation**

Out of **5,000 total applicants:**

* 84% (4,212) are Non-Study Group applicants
* 16% (788) are Study Group applicants

**Interpretation**

Non-Study Group channels currently drive the majority of applicant volume.

While Study Groups contribute fewer applicants, they may still provide targeted or high-quality leads.

**Recommendation**

* Maintain Non-Study Group outreach as the primary acquisition channel
* Conduct deeper analysis of conversion rates to assess the quality of Study Group applicants


## Insight 2: Applications Are Highly Concentrated in Specific Programs

**Observation**

A small number of programs attract the majority of applicants, including programs related to:

* Business Analytics
* Computer Science

**Interpretation**

Interest among applicants is not evenly distributed across academic programs.

**Recommendation**

* Focus marketing and outreach on high-demand programs
* Review low-demand programs for repositioning or targeted marketing strategies


## Insight 3: India Dominates Applicant Country Volume

**Observation**

The highest number of applicants came from:

1. India (~2.7K applicants)
2. United States
3. Ghana
4. Nigeria
5. Pakistan

**Interpretation**

The applicant pool is heavily concentrated in a few countries, particularly India.

**Recommendation**

* Maintain strong engagement with the Indian market
* Expand recruitment efforts in emerging regions such as Africa and Southeast Asia


## Insight 4: Outreach Activity Is High but Completion Rates Are Uneven

**Observation**

Many applicants reach the **"Sent for Evaluation"** stage but fewer progress through later stages.

**Interpretation**

This suggests bottlenecks in:

* Follow-up communication
* Document verification
* Applicant support processes

**Recommendation**

* Improve follow-up workflows
* Introduce automated reminders and application checklists


## Insight 5: Outreach Outcomes Are Skewed Toward Initial Contact

**Observation**

A large number of outreach outcomes fall under:

* "Missing"
* "Not Responded"

Few applicants reach final outcomes.

**Interpretation**

Initial outreach does not consistently convert into engagement.

**Recommendation**

* Improve outreach messaging and communication strategy
* Implement structured follow-up schedules
* Track outreach conversion metrics


## Insight 6: Counselor Performance Is Unevenly Distributed

**Observation**

One counselor handled a significantly larger portion of outreach activities.

**Interpretation**

Workload distribution among counselors is uneven.

**Recommendation**

* Rebalance counselor assignments
* Share best practices from high-performing counselors


## Insight 7: Admission Trends Show a Spike in 2024

**Observation**

Admissions increased sharply in **2024** before stabilizing.

**Interpretation**

The spike may be linked to changes in outreach strategies or external factors.

**Recommendation**

* Investigate outreach strategies used in 2024
* Apply successful approaches to future admission cycles


# Strategic Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategies are recommended:

1. Prioritize high-performing programs and regions while expanding into emerging markets.
2. Focus on outreach effectiveness rather than outreach volume.
3. Improve follow-up communication during the application process.
4. Balance counselor workload to improve efficiency and applicant experience.
5. Use historical admission trends to optimize future recruitment strategies.
